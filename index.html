About

I am a first-year undergraduate student at the University of Liverpool, majoring in Applied Mathematics. Concurrently, I serve as a Research Assistant at PremiLab, supervised by Prof. Qiufeng Wang, where I work on formal mathematical problem-solving systems, agents, and multimodal reasoning of large language models. If you share similar research interests and would like to chat about research or collaboration, please feel free to reach out via email.

In recent months, I have been working on AI4Math, with a focus on strengthening the mathematical reasoning of language models through formal methods. By representing problems in formal languages, reasoning can be grounded in precise, verifiable structures rather than in ambiguous natural-language descriptions. In my spare time, I have begun exploring Mechanistic Interpretability. Compared with applying language models to practical problems, I find probing their internal features a far more fascinating pursuit, one that also helps us better improve their reasoning.
